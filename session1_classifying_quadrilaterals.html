💡 The Four-Sided Family

A quadrilateral is any closed polygon with exactly 4 sides and 4 angles. "Quad" means four, "lateral" means side.

🔑Every quadrilateral also has 4 vertices (corners) and 2 diagonals (lines connecting opposite corners). The interior angles always add up to 360°.
🏷️ Key Properties to Look For
⟷
Parallel Sides
Lines that never meet, always the same distance apart
≡
Equal Sides
Sides that are the same length (shown with tick marks)
⊾
Right Angles
Exactly 90° — shown with a small square in the corner
✕ Diagonals in Quadrilaterals

A diagonal connects two non-adjacent (opposite) vertices. All quadrilaterals have exactly 2 diagonals.

Rectangle — 2 diagonals
Rhombus — 2 diagonals
Trapezoid — 2 diagonals

Square
The most special quad!
Tap to expand ▼
  • All 4 sides are equal in length
  • All 4 angles are 90° (right angles)
  • Both pairs of opposite sides are parallel
  • Diagonals bisect each other at 90°
4 equal sides 4 right angles 2 pairs parallel Also a: rectangle, rhombus, parallelogram
Rectangle
Right angles, 2 pairs equal
Tap to expand ▼
  • Opposite sides are equal in length
  • All 4 angles are 90° (right angles)
  • Both pairs of opposite sides are parallel
  • Diagonals are equal in length and bisect each other
2 pairs equal sides 4 right angles 2 pairs parallel Also a: parallelogram
Rhombus
The "tilted square"
Tap to expand ▼
  • All 4 sides are equal in length
  • Opposite angles are equal (but NOT necessarily 90°)
  • Both pairs of opposite sides are parallel
  • Diagonals bisect each other at 90° but are different lengths
4 equal sides 2 pairs parallel Also a: parallelogram
Parallelogram
Opposite sides parallel
Tap to expand ▼
  • Opposite sides are parallel and equal
  • Opposite angles are equal
  • Adjacent angles add up to 180°
  • Diagonals bisect each other (but are not equal)
2 pairs equal sides 2 pairs parallel
Trapezoid / Trapezium
At least 1 pair parallel
Tap to expand ▼
  • Exactly one pair of parallel sides (called the "bases")
  • The other two sides are called "legs" — they may be equal (isosceles trapezoid) or not
  • Co-interior angles between parallel sides add to 180°
1 pair parallel
🌏In Australia we usually say trapezium. In North America they say trapezoid. Both mean the same shape!
Kite
2 pairs of adjacent equal sides
Tap to expand ▼
  • Two pairs of adjacent sides are equal
  • One pair of opposite angles are equal
  • Diagonals are perpendicular (cross at 90°)
  • One diagonal bisects the other
2 pairs adjacent equal
